Music that I like

Why I like it

Ever since I was a teenager, I've always been fond of slower music. I find it to be a great way to unwind and relax after a long day. The soothing melodies and gentle rhythms help me escape the stresses of daily life. Whether I'm working, studying, or just taking a moment for myself, this type of music creates the perfect atmosphere for reflection and tranquility.

My first love for music came from the band, Pink Floyd. As a teenager and going through my own hardships and rampant hormones, I found them to relay a message that I quite relate to, even to this day. All of Pink Floyd's albums are of their own story, and after listening to their discography numerous of times, I can say that they all build off one another and even reference each other.

I guess Pink Floyd would be the start of my love for slow music. I find slow music to take itself less seriously. I say it's easier to digest the idea of a song when it is slower. I am not saying that fast music is bad,
but I find it to be more of a challenge to listen to.

I find that fast music is more about the beat and the rhythm,
while slow music is more about the lyrics and the message. I find that slow music is more about the journey,
while fast music is more about the destination. I find that slow music is more about the experience,
while fast music is more about the result. I find that slow music is more about the process,
while fast music is more about the product. I find that slow music is more about the art,
